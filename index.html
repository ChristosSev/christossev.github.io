Christos Sevastopoulos
csevasto@uic.edu

I am currently a postdoctoral researcher at the University of Illinois Chicago, in the Richard and Loan Hill Department of Biomedical Engineering. I received my PhD in Computer Engineering from UT Arlington, advised by Filia Makedon. Before this, I completed my Master's in Robotics at the University of Bristol, UK. I earned my Bachelor's degree in Physics at the National & Kapodistrian University of Athens, Greece. Additionally, I have worked as a researcher at NCSR Demokritos under the supervision of Stasinos Konstantopoulos and as a Machine Learning Intern for GN Group .

My research lies at the crossroads of Deep Learning, Computer Vision, and Robotics, with the goal of developing advanced AI systems that improve robotic perception and decision-making. Specifically, I focus on transformer-based architectures and multimodal data to enhance the capabilities of robots in real-world environments. This includes developing techniques for image reconstruction, segmentation, and enhancement in medical imaging using Generative AI methods like diffusion models and GANs. Additionally, my work explores autonomous navigation and traversability estimation for mobile robots, aiming to enable reliable operation in complex, dynamic environments. My goal is to integrate these AI-driven approaches with robotic control systems to create machines that can perform tasks safely, reliably, and efficiently.
